I am an MUEP student at Arizona State University and Graduate Teaching Assistant in the School of Geographical Sciences and Urban Planning, Tempe, Arizona.

I completed my Bachelor of Urban and Regional Planning at KUET, Bangladesh, graduating 2nd of 69 students (CGPA 3.83/4.0) with two consecutive Dean's Awards. My work spans air quality modeling, aerosol retrievals, remote sensing, wildfire emissions, and community-scale environmental health.

I have co-authored peer-reviewed publications in remote sensing and environmental science, contributed to UGC-funded flood modeling, and coordinated geospatial operations for the World Food Programme. I co-founded the Center for Environmental Research (CER) to mentor early-career researchers.
